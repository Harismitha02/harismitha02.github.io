Food Delivery Funnel Insights

Python | Tableau | Excel

Analyzed 45K+ delivery records using Python, Excel, and Tableau to uncover trends in delays, weather impact, and city-wise delivery times. Identified 74% spike in festival-day delays and lower ratings during sunny conditions to guide operational improvements.

Here are the questions I was interested in answering:

1) What is the average delivery rating?
2) What are the most common weather conditions during delays?
3) Does traffic impact delivery time?
4) What is the impact of festivals on delivery delays?
5) What are the most common times for delays?
6) How does weather affect customer ratings?
7) At what times of day are orders at their peak in each city?
8) How are delivery times and weather conditions distributed across the country?
9) Which cities experience the highest average delivery times, and during which hours?
10) How do traffic and weather jointly impact delivery efficiency and customer satisfaction?

I took the following steps to create my analysis:

1) Preprocessing & ETL (Python): Cleaned and transformed raw delivery data using pandas — handled null values, standardized columns, merged date-time fields, and engineered features such as delivery delay duration, order hour, and weather impact flags.

2) Analysis & Metrics (Advanced Excel): Conducted deep-dive analysis using pivot tables, GETPIVOTDATA, conditional formulas, and statistical functions (like MAX, MODE.SNGL) to extract key insights on delivery time, traffic impact, weather patterns, and festival influence.
3) Interactive Visualization (Tableau): Built a dynamic dashboard with visual filters (slicers for city, festival, weather), KPI cards, bar and line charts, and geographic symbol maps — allowing stakeholders to explore delivery trends by time, location, and external conditions.

Here are my key takeaways:
  • The average delivery time was 26 minutes, with a maximum delay reaching up to 54 minutes.
  • Fog was the most common weather condition observed during delayed deliveries.
  • Traffic congestion was a major contributor to delivery delays, especially in metro areas during peak hours.
  • City-wise trends revealed that delivery performance varied sharply by location and hour, with some cities peaking in orders late at night.
  • Semi-urban regions saw the majority of orders post-12 PM, and these areas also had higher average delivery times than metropolitan cities.
  • Bad traffic and weather conditions consistently contributed to delays, especially during evening hours in metro cities.
  • Customer ratings dropped by over 13% during sunny conditions, highlighting a potential risk to satisfaction during adverse weather.
  • Wednesdays had the longest average delivery times, while Saturdays during festivals experienced even greater delays.
  • The most common delays occurred between 6 PM to 9 PM, reinforcing the importance of operational efficiency during dinner-time orders.
